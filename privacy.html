Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 16, 2026

Pickr ("we", "our", or "the app") is a free photo-selection tool. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your choices.

1. Data We Do Not Collect

Pickr does not have a backend server. We do not collect, store, or transmit any of the following to our systems:

  • Your photos or image files
  • Your name, email address, or account information
  • Your browsing history or behavior on other sites

2. Local Folder Mode

When you open a local folder, all processing happens entirely inside your browser. Your files never leave your device. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.

3. Google Drive and Google Photos (Cloud Mode)

When you connect to Google Drive or Google Photos, Pickr uses the Google OAuth 2.0 flow to obtain a temporary access token. This token is stored only in your browser's memory for the duration of your session and is never sent to our servers.

We request the following Google OAuth scopes:

  • drive.readonly — to list and read images from your chosen Drive folder
  • drive.file — to create a "selected photos" output folder and upload selected images back to your Drive
  • photoslibrary.readonly — to list your albums and load photo thumbnails from Google Photos

Pickr accesses only the specific folder or album you choose. We do not read, modify, or delete any other files in your Google account.

Google's own privacy policy applies to how Google handles your data: policies.google.com/privacy

4. Google API Client ID

If you enter a Google OAuth Client ID during setup, it is saved in your browser's localStorage on your device only. It is never transmitted to our servers.

5. Cookies and Tracking

Pickr does not use cookies or any tracking technologies for advertising or analytics purposes at this time.

6. Third-Party Services

Pickr loads the following third-party scripts:

  • Google Identity Services (accounts.google.com/gsi/client) — for Google OAuth authentication
  • JSZip (Cloudflare CDN) — for creating ZIP file downloads in browsers that don't support direct disk access

These scripts are subject to their own privacy policies.

7. Children's Privacy

Pickr is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of Pickr after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance.

9. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
